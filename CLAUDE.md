# bushglue

## Agent contribution norms

This repo is maintained by one meatbag at a time. Agent contributions land here from multiple sources. To keep the signal-to-decoration ratio high:

- **Default to updating existing docs only as necessary.** If the change is self-evident from the diff + commit message, don't write a 1-pager. Focus on user-facing critical info only.
- **One doc per change, not a fan-out.** No separate 1-pager + walkthrough + full review + amendment for the same work. Pick one.
- **Don't invent decisions for a fictitious team.** There is one meatbag reading. Address them directly. No "the team needs to decide X" sections, no "Ask:" closers, no rhetorical decision lists.
- **Risk/severity tables** only when there are more than three real risks. Two risks fit in two sentences.
- **Write to the operator, not to a stakeholder.** Skip the executive-summary register. Plain technical prose.

## Valve control — where it stands (2026-06-10)

On `main`: the valve node is back on **CAN** — a small fleet, all CircuitPython sharing one `valve.py` CAN driver (the SERVO42D closed-loop driver, restored from `6e1c03f`), differing only in host link + CAN carrier. Three node types: **Pico 2 W + CanBerry MCP2515** over Wi-Fi/MQTT (`code.py`, `BOARD="canberry"`, SPI GP6/7/4/5, 16 MHz; now also takes **multiple Wi-Fi networks** `NETWORKS` + **multiple brokers** `MQTT_BROKERS`, tried before the /24 scan); **XIAO nRF52840 + MCP2515** over BLE → `bush_valve_ble` (`code_xiao_ble.py`); and **Waveshare RP2350-CAN** (onboard XL2515, no radio) over **USB-serial → the new `bush_valve_serial` bridge** (`code_usb_serial.py` + `boot.py`). Still deferred (no hardware): a reflashed **BridgePlate + CanBerry** centerpiece, also USB-serial (Pi-Plates coexistence rules in the plan). Motor link is CAN-only; UART and STEP/DIR are retired (recover step/dir from `3f40ee8`, UART from `9c709f5^`). 42D menu: CAN 500 kbps, ID 1, MStep 16 — the firmware sets SR_vFOC + run current over the bus (there is no "CR_CAN" work mode). Protocol + host-link table in `firmware/valve-control/PROTOCOL.md`.

**Bench status (2026-06-10): full CAN closed loop PROVEN with the 42D motor on the Waveshare node.** `0x31` encoder reads (live during motion AND while stalled), `init()` ACKs, motion, and gentle seat homing all work. The bring-up blocker was **CAN bus termination** — with no 120 Ω terminator the XL2515 can't even transmit (TXREQ stuck, TXERR=1, TEC frozen at 0 — distinct from the no-ACK/TEC-climb case); one terminator (the 42D end) fixed it instantly. Verified + locked into `valve.py`: **`DIR_TOWARD_OPEN=0x00` / `DIR_TOWARD_CLOSED=0x80`** (the old guesses were inverted), `_enc_sign=-1`; **current = torque** (`0x83`, the only knob — no separate torque cmd on the 42D), `VALVE_CURRENT_MA=1000` for fast moves; **lockrotor protection kept ON** (`init` sends PROTECT `0x01`) as the seat detector (`0x3E` latch) + operating jam net — it does NOT false-trip at adequate current, and `0xF1` gives run status; **full travel ~3.76 rev, `OPEN_STEPS=11200`**. (Flashed CP 10.2.1 `raspberry_pi_pico2`; XL2515 16 MHz; SIT65HVD230 3.3 V.) The **Pico 2 W + CanBerry and XIAO** builds gave bench trouble and are NOT yet proven. CircuitPython gotchas fixed: no `del` on `bytearray` (use `_rx[:] = _rx[n:]`); writing `code.py` auto-reloads AND a manual Ctrl-D reloads = **double run** (enter the REPL first for one-shot motion probes).

**Homing is ENABLED + bench-proven, and deliberately GENTLE** (`HOMING_DISABLED=False`): `cmd_home()` blocking protection-seek — drive toward the closed seat at LOW current (300 mA), the 42D latches `0x3E` at the seat, back off a margin, zero. **It must NEVER bump current to force past resistance — forcing shreds the valve internals** (that grinding made the current bench valve's sticky spots), so rough homing perpetuates the damage; a worn valve errors `home_stuck` instead of being pushed. The **current bench valve is too chewed to home gently** (moves only when forced to 800–1000 mA) → it needs replacing; do NOT raise the homing current to compensate. Safety asymmetry (operator-corrected 2026-06-13 — the docs had the hazard inverted): the **CLOSED seat is the dangerous stop, not the open one.** The motor pushes *through* the closed seat (~1 microstep of give at 800 mA), so forcing toward it grinds the needle in and **permanently shreds the valve** (what chewed this one). The **OPEN hard stop merely jams the motor harmlessly** — it stalls, latches lockrotor, frees by reversing toward closed: a recoverable nuisance, **no margin needed**. So never ram the closed seat (gentle homing, ram-protected low end); the open stop is a fine backstop and `OPEN_STEPS`' ~540-step open margin is not a safety requirement. Bench zero is now homed **at the seat** (true-closure ground truth) — operate forward from 0, never drive hard back to it. Next homing avenue: the 42D's own internal homing (`0x90`/`0x91`/`0x92`, watch `0xF1`/`0x3E`; mind the NVM auto-home-rams-on-boot gotcha). More in the MKS-quirks and closed-seat memories.

**Characterization instrumentation (2026-06-11, pre-envelope-sweep):** the firmware now has a runtime `limits` topic (move_rpm/move_acc/stream_max_rpm/breath_max_rpm), per-move `moved` telemetry (duration + commanded-vs-encoder), an in-flight `trace` encoder poll, a 0x3E stall watch for the silent 0xF6 modes (`stream_stalled`/`breath_stalled` fail loudly instead of dead-reckoning past a stopped motor), rpm-aware move timeouts, and `home here` (bench zero without seat contact — the chewed bench valve can't seat-home). `utils/bush-valve-sweep` (direct data-CDC, no broker) runs the mid-range RPM×accel grid and synthetic-stream tests; topic table in `firmware/valve-control/PROTOCOL.md`.

**Envelope sweep results (2026-06-11, bench, chewed valve, 1–1.6 rev window moves; CSVs in `firmware/valve-control/bench-data/2026-06-11/`):** acc ≤ 192 is ramp-bound (~95 eff rpm regardless of commanded speed — rpm cells above ~160 are duplicates). At acc 250: **12 V ≈ 180 eff rpm reliable @ 1000 mA, ≈ 215 clean @ 1500; 24 V ≈ 230 deterministic @ 1500, ≈ 295 but ±25% move-time variance @ 1000.** Raising current buys determinism, not top speed (higher current target = bigger FOC voltage vector = bus saturates at lower rpm) → **operating point: 24 V + 1500 mA.** Decel overshoot ≈ 40 steps at acc 250 (≈ 10 at 192), invariant with current — every direction reversal pays it. Streaming: the 0.2–1.5 Hz chirp tracks within 1–2 samples of pipeline lag (mean ≈ 150 steps, no stalls); the square test found a **seat-ram bug, now fixed** — a clamped edge teleported `motor_pos_steps` to the target, the next flat sample commanded speed-0 at BREATH_ACC (acc 8 ≈ 7 s decel from 600 rpm), and the motor coasted ~2 rev into the closed seat at ~580 rpm before the 0x3E watch latched. `_service_stream` now dead-reckons only the clamped per-tick coverage (catch-up continues across later samples) and snap-stops (acc 0) holds/underruns; square flats now hold ±80 steps, edges slew at `stream_max_rpm` by design. Sweep-tool side: streams never self-correct an initial offset, so `bush-valve-sweep stream` pre-positions at sample[0] (the firmware's stream-seed read now also re-grounds at start). The old clamp wart (off-window positions stored clamped → lying `moved.enc`, inch-up recovery) is FIXED: bookkeeping is unclamped truth, targets clamp at the control layer, off-window recovery moves compute true deltas.

**Stream slew escalation (2026-06-11, same bench):** the positioned-move "ceilings" above are an artifact of the 42D's 0xFD profile execution, NOT the motor — raw 0xF6 speed-follow (the bush-cue path) tracks **~8× faster**. Escalating chirps at 24 V / 1500 mA (`bush-valve-sweep stream --max-rpm --current`): peak slews of 452/660/905/1108/1527 rpm all tracked with error exactly lag-proportional, zero signed drift, zero stalls; the knee is between **1527 (fully faithful) and 1866 rpm** (monotonic ~25% signed shed at the 5.5 Hz/30 S/s end — motor vs sample-quantization indistinguishable, and production streams 30 S/s anyway). Practical stream budget on the production span (peak slew = 2πf·A): **full-span p-t-p to ~2.3 Hz, half-span at ~4.5 Hz, quarter-span at ~9 Hz**, sample rate (Nyquist 15 Hz) and flame/plumbing low-pass binding above that. `stream_max_rpm` default 600 is very conservative — 1500 is bench-justified at 24 V/1500 mA (keep 600 until the regulator/flame side is characterized; the limits topic sets it at runtime).

**High-frequency map (2026-06-12, bench-data/2026-06-12/):** stream rates 60 and 100 S/s work (finer sampling *reduces* lag error — the control chirp halved it vs 30 S/s). The drivetrain has an **amplitude-dependent resonance in the 8–15+ Hz band**: gain 1.21 @ 8 Hz/±1280, 1.76 @ 12 Hz/±832, but 2.7–3.9 @ 10–15 Hz at small (±200–256) amplitudes — small commands ring hardest (suspect dog-clutch free play, meaning motor-encoder swing ≠ valve swing up there), no peak found below Nyquist, and **large amplitude ≥13 Hz goes unstable** (the 15 Hz/±640 run walked into the open stop — that incident also revealed the lockrotor latch can set silently post-stream, surfacing as an instant ~7 ms pseudo-stall on the next move, self-healed by the stall handlers). Operating doctrine: **DC–8 Hz precision band** (gain ≈1, phase ≈20 ms·2πf, correctable); **8–12 Hz boost band** (gain 1.2–1.8 at flame-relevant amplitudes, budget it); **>12 Hz fenced by publisher band-limiting** — the stream runaway guard is the net, not the fence.

**Production motor supply DECIDED (2026-06-11): Mean Well DDR-60G-24** (24 V / 2.5 A / 60 W, 9–36 V in) — exactly the bench envelope the whole characterization ran under, so all numbers above carry over; budget ~6 A upstream at 12 V input. One gap it created: DDR-60 overload is **hiccup mode** (output drops + retries), and a 42D brownout is **silent** — encoder accumulator resets, volatile init (SR_vFOC, 0x83 current) reverts, while the MCU keeps claiming `homed: true` against a now-false zero (bench-observed twice today via power cycling). **Closed by ground-loss protection**: every pre/post-motion encoder read (target sync, move/nudge end, breath cycle, stream seed) checks encoder vs bookkeeping; > 1 rev of disagreement → `ground_lost` (un-home, error, config re-asserted, re-home required). Bonus: the stream seed read re-grounds at stream start, so streams self-correct initial offsets instead of inheriting them. Details in `PROTOCOL.md`.

**Audio-rate oscillation + the rectified-walk fix (2026-06-13, bench-data/2026-06-13/, chewed valve, 24 V/1500 mA).** Goal: drive the flame as an audible tone over the 0–0.1 = 0–100% window (≈⅓ turn; full-depth audio ≈ ±560 steps). Two walls, now both addressed: **(1) Torsional resonance** (encoder magnet is on the MOTOR shaft, so the gain is real motor overshoot, not backlash). **Stiffening the bracket moved the peak ~11–12 → ~17–19 Hz** (gain ~3.5 at the small operating amplitude — small commands ring hardest, so this is the real operating gain). Slew is NOT the limit. There is a usable **gap above the peak: ~20–21 Hz reads gain ~1.9 mid-travel; a sustained 21 Hz audible tone held 20 s clean** (DC stable, 0 stalls) — you can "hang out" between the peak and the next mode. **(2) Rectified downward walk into the closed seat** — the open-loop 0xF6 stream dead-reckons between encoder reads, resonance phase-lag rectifies into a silent per-cycle DC drift that parks the motor in the seat with NO stall latched. **FIX (committed 5f44ece): in-stream DC re-grounding** in `valve.py` `_on_trace_read` — each 50 ms guard-poll leak-pulls `motor_pos_steps` 1/`stream_reground_div` toward the true encoder pos (default 16, τ≈0.8 s, 0=off, runtime-tunable via the `limits` topic). Leaky not snap: rejects the per-cycle swing, feeds back only the DC walk, fast trajectory stays open-loop. A/B-validated: OFF walked the center 743→3450→−172 and runaway-stalled past the seat (err −2069); ON held center ±150 steps across the band, 0 stalls, gain preserved (err −56). Caveat: **near the seat the chewed valve rings harder (gain 2.9 vs 1.9) — near-seat gap operation can't be validated on this worn valve; needs the replacement** (expect ~1.9 to hold near-seat on an undamaged valve). Full detail in the [[project_valve_driveline_resonance]] memory.

**New valve swapped in + home backoff bug fixed 2026-06-23.** The original gentle home (0xFD MOVE_POS toward open after a seat latch) silently did not move the motor on this tight-seal valve no matter the recovery sequence — five `home_stuck` retries at 400/800/1500 mA with protect on/off all yielded 0–10 raw counts of backoff motion, yet a manual nudge at 800 mA from idle broke the seal cleanly. REPL-driven diagnostic at the harmless open-stop lockrotor surrogate pinpointed two things: (a) post-lockrotor 0xFD silently refuses to move (the 42D's commanded-position state blocks it — exact mechanism still unknown, not lockrotor re-arm, not current); (b) 0xF6 CONSTANT_SPEED from the same state moves cleanly, but takes ~1 s after the command before the motor actually starts ramping. **Fix:** new `cmd_home` backoff is RELEASE_PROT + SET_PROTECT 0x00 + full ENABLE 0→1 power-cycle (matches the idle→nudge path) + HOME_BACKOFF_CUR (now 800 mA, was 400) + 0xF6 toward open + poll-encoder-until-HOME_BACKOFF_STEPS-reached (4 s timeout) + 0xF6 spd=0 stop + ENABLE 0 + SET_PROTECT 0x01 re-arm. Two consecutive real homes from different start positions completed in ~6.6 s each, idle/homed:true/pos=0 (seat-anchored zero). **Bench-measured new valve `open_steps = 11076 µsteps = 3.46 rev`** (default 11200 was close but ~1.1% over) — set via `calibrate 11076`. Bridge re-enabled, MQTT path verified, valve parked at target 0.5 (pos 0.499); default breath (amp 0.04) keeps the valley clear of the seat as long as target ≥ 0.08.
